Voice therapy

Plain-language info about what it is, who it’s for, and what to expect.

What is voice therapy?

Voice therapy is guided work on how you use your voice—breath, pitch, resonance, and how you want to sound day to day. For trans and gender-diverse people, it’s often about finding a voice that feels more like you and that you can use comfortably in the world.

Who is it for?

Anyone who wants to work on their voice in a supportive, professional setting. My practice is focused on trans patients: I work with people who are questioning, transitioning, or simply want their voice to better match their identity and goals.

What to expect

Sessions are one-on-one. We’ll talk about what you want to achieve, then work through exercises and practice that fit your goals. Progress depends on you and your body; we go at your pace. You’re never pushed to sound a certain way—we work toward what feels right for you.

How it helps

Clients often say they feel more confident using their voice in daily life, at work, or in social situations. The work can support both the physical side of voice (pitch, resonance) and the emotional side (comfort, safety, expression).
